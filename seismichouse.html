China is located in the Pacific Rim seismic belt, a region with frequent earthquake disasters. The most recent major earthquake occurred in Wenchuan in 2008. Accord- ing to data from the China Earthquake Administration, the surface wave magnitude of the 5.12 Wenchuan earthquake was 8.0. The earthquake affected most of China and many countries and regions in Asia. The earthquake was felt in China from Inner Mongolia in the north, to Shanghai in the east, Tibet in the west, and Hong Kong and Taiwan in the south. Countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, the Philippines, and Japan outside of China also felt the tremor. The Wenchuan earthquake caused huge losses to life and property.
